Overview

The challenge

L&T Realty is one of India's most premium real estate brands. Their website needed to reflect that positioning while serving a wide range of users — from first-time home buyers to seasoned investors.

The challenge was designing an information architecture that handled the complexity of multiple property types, locations, and customer journeys without overwhelming users — and delivering a UI premium enough to match the brand.

🎯
The Problem
A complex real estate platform with multiple property types and user journeys — the existing IA made discovery difficult and the UI didn't match the premium brand positioning.
✅
The Goal
Design a complete revamp — clear IA, high-fidelity prototypes, and a premium UI that reflects L&T's brand and converts property browsers into enquiries.
Process

How I approached it

1
Brand & User Research
Researched L&T Realty's positioning, competitor real estate websites, and user mental models — how people search for premium property and what builds trust.
2
Information Architecture
Redesigned the full IA — structuring properties by type, location, and buyer intent, ensuring every user type could find what they need within 2 clicks.
3
UX Planning & Wireframes
Mapped the complete user journey and built wireframes for all key pages — homepage, property listings, project detail pages, and enquiry flows.
4
Prototyping
Built interactive Figma prototypes for key user journeys — tested internally before moving to high-fidelity visual design.
5
High-Fidelity UI Design
Delivered polished UI with a premium visual language — sophisticated typography, refined colour palette, and high-quality imagery treatment befitting the L&T brand.

Learnings

What I learned

Enterprise design requires balancing brand heritage with modern UX — the visual language must feel familiar to existing users while attracting new ones.
A component library is an investment, not just a deliverable — every hour spent building it saves ten hours on future pages and keeps the product consistent over time.
Large organisations move slowly — being proactive about communicating design decisions and rationale keeps projects moving even when stakeholder availability is limited.
