Overview

The challenge

Shriram Life Insurance needed a design partner who could own the full digital design process — not just execute briefs, but proactively identify UX improvements using real analytics data and manage stakeholder relationships independently.

The role required designing mobile-first landing pages, plan pages, campaign pages, and website enhancements that balance business goals with user trust — critical in a high-stakes insurance context.

🎯
The Problem
Insurance websites are complex — multiple user types, regulatory requirements, and high-stakes decisions. Design had to build trust while driving conversions on every page.
✅
The Goal
Own end-to-end digital design, leverage Google Analytics for continuous UX improvement, and manage stakeholder relationships independently as Lead Senior Designer.
Process

How I approached it

1
Analytics-First Thinking
Started with Google Analytics data before designing a single screen — understanding drop-off points, traffic sources, and user behaviour patterns to inform every design decision.
2
Stakeholder Management
Led regular stakeholder calls, gathered requirements independently, and translated business needs into clear design briefs — functioning as a design lead, not just an executor.
3
Mobile-First Design
Designed all pages mobile-first — landing pages, plan comparison pages, and campaign pages — since the majority of insurance traffic comes from mobile devices.
4
Trust-First Visual Language
Applied design principles specific to financial products — clear hierarchy, credibility signals, simplified copy, and strong CTAs without feeling aggressive or salesy.
5
Continuous Optimisation
Used post-launch analytics data to iterate on underperforming sections, improving conversion rates and engagement over successive design cycles.

Learnings

What I learned

Analytics before design is non-negotiable — understanding where users drop off is more valuable than any assumption about what they need.
In insurance design, trust signals above the fold are the single most important conversion lever. Users decide within seconds whether a page feels credible.
Owning stakeholder relationships as a designer — not just receiving briefs — leads to significantly better design outcomes and stronger client partnerships.
